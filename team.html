What Do All These Qualifications Mean?

We know the alphabet soup of financial qualifications can be confusing. Here's what they actually mean:

CFP® (Certified Financial Planner)

The gold standard in financial planning. CFP® professionals have completed extensive training, passed rigorous exams, and committed to ongoing education and ethical standards. Only the top 15% of advisors hold this qualification.

DipPFS (Diploma in Financial Planning)

The professional standard for financial advisors. This advanced qualification covers pensions, investments, taxation, and estate planning in depth. It's the stepping stone to becoming Chartered.

CertPFS (Certificate in Financial Planning)

The foundation qualification for financial advisors. Anyone giving regulated financial advice must hold at least this level, which covers core financial planning principles and regulatory requirements.

FCA Regulation

All our advisors are registered with the Financial Conduct Authority – the UK's financial regulator. This means we meet strict professional standards, and your money is protected by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme.
